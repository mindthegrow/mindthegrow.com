Location

REQUIREMENTS

  1. No residential zones.

  2. Must be at least 1000ft from schools, child care locations, city-owned recreational centers, or drug/alcohol treatment facilities.

  3. CCEs must be established in an area which will give community support to the existence and operation of the enterprise (see below).

  4. Proof of possession of the premises where the CCE is located with a valid zone-use permit and most recent certificate of occupancy.

  5. You cannot sell, provide, transfer or distribute cannabis for renumeration. Not inside, not outside. Just no. You must not allow outdoor smoking at street level in a location visible from a public right-of-way or where children congregate.

  6. Operating hours are prohibited from occurring during the the hours of 2am to 7am.

  7. No person may be in possession of more than one ounce of cannabis. No liquor, either. You may not hold a liquor license and designated consumption area license on the same premises.

  8. No “conspicuous advertising.” You must seek community support for locations where you want to advertise your business.

CHALLENGES

  1. You must have a location before applying. You may have to sit on your facility for an uncertain amount of time as the application gets processed.

  2. You must find a location that meets all the requirements in the code and is also located in close proximity to an existing recreational dispensary where your customers may legally procure products for consumption on your premises.

  3. For ease of business, nearby dispensaries should be willing and able to partner with your enterprise in the form of deals, sponsorships, workshops, etc. to entice customers. Scoping out potential partnerships is a time-consuming process that must be included in the real estate search.

  4. Even after securing a location and partnerships, the application may be denied or delayed pending further action, so you must have existing capital to endure the process.
